Project 1: Google Play Store 2018 EDA

A Shiny Application that investigates the Google Play Store from 2018

PROJECTS

Project 1: Google Play Store 2018 EDA

By Kristin Teves

October 7, 2020

Background

Google Play Store consists of numerous applications within varying categories. As a digital distribution service for Android, users have access to a number of family, gaming, and tool apps, to name a few. There were approximately 2.7 million applications available as of February 2017. The scraped Kaggle data reveals a sample of the Google Play Store as of 2018. The dataset reveals 33 categories in the Google Play Store. Further exploration and analysis can be done on the Shiny app to help the organization determine which categories contain the most or least amount of apps, installations, rating distributions, and more.

Overview and Ranking

At first glance, we see that across all apps in the Google Play Store, the average rating is a little over 4.0, indicating a large number of highly rated apps. Although there is a left-skewed distribution, the average ratings still vary at the category level.

Despite there being more Family apps, as seen in the Top 10 Made bar chart, Top 10 Installs reveal Game apps as the most installed category, followed by Communication and Tools. Therefore, from a business point of view, considering which category is heavily saturated, like Family apps, is something to recognize when thinking about competition and competitors in the market. In addition, recognizing the categories that are installed the most may help entreprenuers decide which categories gain the most attention and installations from users.

Category Number of Apps Installs
FAMILY 1877 6,237,542,505
GAME 946 13,457,924,415
TOOLS 830 8,112,771,915
PRODUCTIVITY 374 5,793,091,369

Of the categories that are made and installed the most, Family, Game, Tools, and Productivity fall under both these top 10 lists.

Category Details

The Shiny app gives the organization the ability to select various categories to compare boxplot ratings or stacked bar charts of free vs. paid apps, among others, to compare between different categories.

Taking a look at the top 3 category apps made–Family, Game, and Tools–we see that most of these apps are Free, with a median paid price of $2.99 for those that are not. Together, these categories consist of 27.81 billion installations from the Google Play Store.

Between the top 3 category apps made, most apps contain content rating accessible for Everyone. There is a notable Teen rating presence between Family and Game apps. In all, having more apps available for everyone could have led to the high number of installations from users.
